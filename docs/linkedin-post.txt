Built a new end-to-end Power BI portfolio project: Sales Performance Analytics.

The goal was to go beyond a simple dashboard and create something that feels closer to a real business case. I structured the project with reproducible synthetic data, a star schema, a DAX KPI library, a dashboard blueprint, and documentation designed for both technical review and executive storytelling.

The dataset covers two years of transactional sales across regions, channels, categories, products, and customer segments, plus a separate target table for actual-versus-plan analysis. A few of the insights that emerged:

- Sales grew nearly 13% year over year, but profit grew more slowly, which compressed margin.
- Direct Sales delivered better margin and profit per order than Online, despite lower total volume.
- Some product lines generated strong revenue but weak margin, making them clear pricing-review candidates.

What I liked most about this build was treating it as a complete analytics project rather than only a visualization exercise: data generation, modeling, DAX, design decisions, and portfolio packaging all had to work together.

Tech used: Power BI, DAX, Power Query, Python, dimensional modeling, GitHub documentation.
